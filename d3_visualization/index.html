About

Carta serves companies who manage their equity and the stakeholders who own that equity.

As more startups use Carta, more stakeholders gain exposure to the software and, as stakeholders become aware of the platform's advantages, more stakeholders refer companies they have a stake in to use Carta.

This visualization seeks to give insight into that process, both in terms of the players involved and the sheer power of exponential growth for Carta's marketplace acquisition.

Assumptions

For the sake of ease, I make a few simplifying assumptions.

  • I focus on investors and accelerators as I hypothesize they are the largest drivers of referrals. Other groups, like serial entrepreneaurs, tax and legal firms and employees in the startup ecosystem are also valuable.
  • I simplify the process for adding referral partners. I assume that every time a company switches to Carta, every investor with a stake in that company has an independent probability of becoming a partner. A further tuned model may consider the proportion of their portfolio that already uses Carta.
  • I assume it takes time to work with a partner to add their clients to Carta. I assume that we are able to convert 25% of their portfolio each quarter and, if we have more than 75% of their existing portfolio, then we convert the rest of their investments to Carta.
  • I assume that investors fall into three groups: active adopters, passive adopters and never adopters. Active adopters get it. They have a 50/50 chance of becoming a partner each time one of their portfolio companies switches to Carta. Passive adopters are open to change, though more apprehensive with a 10% chance each time a portfolio company switches to Carta and, never adopters, are curmedgeons with a 0% chance. The groups are split 20%, 60%, 20%, respectively.

Source

This simulation runs off the Crunchbase database snapshot, a replica of their database as of December 31, 2013.
